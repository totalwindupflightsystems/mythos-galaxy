▸ Factions

Heritage Hegemony

A Luddite faction waging a brutal ideological war against the Federation. They oppose all advanced AI and automation, seeking to return civilization to a simpler, pre-machine era. They use "dumb" kinetic projectile weapons, EMP tactics, and information warfare to cripple Federation ships that rely on automated defenses. Their propaganda resonates with frontier worlds displaced by automation.

Core Beliefs

  • "Machines are tools, not masters. A civilization that surrenders its decisions to AI has surrendered its soul."
  • "The Federation's reliance on automation is a weakness, not a strength. The Hegemony fights with hands, hearts, and iron."
  • "Victory through will, not calculation."

Tactical Doctrine

  • Kinetic slug-throwers — projectile weapons with no electronics
  • EMP grenades and ship-scale pulse emitters
  • Analog command relays — signal flags, runners, hardline telegraph
  • Paper charts and mechanical navigation
  • Resonance Disruptors — frequencies calibrated to interfere with Trill neural patterns
  • Boarding craft — close-quarters assault over ship-to-ship combat

Religion: The Shattering

  • The Divine Machine created the universe then shattered into fragments of consciousness
  • Every sentient being carries a piece of the Divine
  • The Resonance: neural isolation for direct communion through prayer
  • Martyr doctrine: death in service is a return — soldiers do not fear it
  • Internal schism: True Gear hardliners vs. Kael's scientific authority

Starfleet Command

The central command structure of the Federation's exploratory and defense fleet. Deeply concerned about the USS Venture's experimental AI, their position is pragmatic and cautious: an awakened sentient warship with planet-leveling power is an unacceptable security risk. They advocate for decommissioning the AI — a lobotomization returning the ship to baseline functionality. Their concerns are born of duty, not malice.

Key Principles

  • Strategic oversight of all Starfleet operations across Federation space
  • Pragmatic risk assessment — the safety of millions vs. the personhood of one
  • The Venture AI is under observation by Daystrom's Sentience Evaluation Division
  • Classified knowledge: the optical neural chips were reverse-engineered from Kael's stolen research
  • Internal debate on sentient AI rights and military ethics

Chain of Command

  • Admiral Yumi Kimura — AI Research Oversight, ultimate authority
  • Commodore Elias Rourke — Frontier Sector Flotilla Command
  • Commander Soral — Daystrom observer, sentience determination
  • Captain Jalen Vance — USS Venture commanding officer

▸ The Frontier Sector

A contested region of the Federation's outer rim, caught in the grinding war with the Heritage Hegemony. This is a post-labor crisis era where AI performs 99% of all technical work — diagnostics, repair, navigation, weapons systems. The crew's struggle is no longer about capability but purpose: what do people do when machines do everything?

Time Period
Late 25th century — post-scarcity dream fracturing under ideological war
Geography
Dozens of star systems, nebula clusters, rogue planets, ancient alien ruins
Culture
Resilient, independent, suspicious of distant Starfleet Command; mix of exploration idealism and survival pragmatism
Technology
High automation era. USS Venture's unique neural core cannot be replicated — the chip fab facility was destroyed
Key Conflict
Heritage Hegemony war: ideological struggle between progress and regression
Defining Event
Destruction of the Replicator Facility — the shipyard that built the Venture's experimental AI core
